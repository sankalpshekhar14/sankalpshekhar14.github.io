Sankalp Shekhar

I am Sankalp Shekhar. I am a Sophomore studying Computer Science and Engineering at Manipal Institute of Technology. I was fascinated with electronics, and code at an early age. Focused more on the hardware part first, I spent time through middle school making small projects like plant watering system, automatic garbage bins, PAPI Lights, with Arduino and Raspberry Pi. I learnt C++ in high school, and for my final project, created a cricket simulation game in Turbo C.

In Junior year, I was introduced to Machine Learning and subsequently Deep Learning, and the field of Computer Vision, intrigued me. I am a part of the AI and Robotics club in my university called Project Manas, which aims to develop a self-driving car for Indian roads. We were also the winning team at IGVC(Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition) 2019.

My first internship was at the Material Sciences lab at CSR(Consortium for Scientific Research), where I developed a Python module called Thermos, which can process thermograms from open datasets, and gain more insights from it. Last winter, I interned at Ernst and Young LLP, where I worked on Document Analysis and Recognition, and digitization of Invoices and Shipping Bills.

I had been reading up a lot Game Playing Algorithms and Chess Engines,and made an algorithm to play a game called ElectroSheep, where the task was to corner sheep using a dog, while avoiding skunks. I used concepts of Behaviorial Planning, and Finite State Machines, to define discrete behaviours for the dog, with only the image frames of the game as the input.
